## Your Three Options

There are three practical ways to get from Incheon Airport (ICN) to central Seoul. Taxis exist but cost $60–90+ and are not worth it for most travelers. Here is how the three real options compare.

## AREX Train — Best for Most Travelers

The AREX (Airport Railroad Express) connects Incheon Airport directly to Seoul Station. There are two services on the same line: All-Stop and Express. Both depart from the same underground platform at ICN Terminal 1.

### AREX All-Stop

The All-Stop train is the one most travelers should take. It costs under 5,000 KRW (a few dollars) and stops at every station along the way, arriving at Seoul Station in about an hour. From Seoul Station, you can transfer to subway Lines 1 and 4 to reach Myeongdong, Jongno, Gangnam, or anywhere else.

The All-Stop train also stops at Hongik University Station (Line 2), which means you can exit there if you are staying in Hongdae — no transfer needed. This is about 50 minutes from ICN.

**Why it is the best default:**
- Cheapest option by far (a few dollars)
- Runs every 6–12 minutes, so you never wait long
- Uses a T-money card or single-use ticket (no advance booking needed)
- Connects directly to Seoul's subway system

### AREX Express

The Express train runs nonstop from ICN to Seoul Station in about 43 minutes. It costs around 9,500 KRW (~$7) and has wider seats, luggage racks, and more space. It runs roughly every 30–40 minutes.

**When it makes sense:** If you arrive during rush hour and want a guaranteed seat with space for luggage, the Express is a comfortable upgrade. The 15-minute time savings is real but not critical for most people. If the next Express departure is 25 minutes away and an All-Stop is leaving in 3 minutes, take the All-Stop — you will arrive sooner.

### How to Find the AREX Platform

1. After clearing immigration and customs, follow the signs to "Airport Railroad" or "AREX" in the arrivals hall
2. Take the escalator or elevator down to the underground transit center (B1 level)
3. Buy a single-use ticket from the machines (they accept cash and cards, and have an English option) or tap your T-money card
4. Board the train — All-Stop and Express use the same platform but different tracks, so check the electronic display

If you are arriving at **Terminal 2**, the AREX also stops there. Terminal 2 is the first stop, and the train continues to Terminal 1 before heading to Seoul. Add about 6 minutes to the travel time.

## Airport Bus — Best for Hotels Not Near a Subway Station

Airport limousine buses run from ICN to specific hotel districts and neighborhoods across Seoul. They typically cost around 15,000–18,000 KRW (~$12–14) and take 60–90 minutes depending on traffic and your stop.

**When it makes sense:** Airport buses are useful if your hotel is not within walking distance of a subway station, or if you are carrying heavy luggage and want to avoid navigating subway stairs and transfers. Some buses stop directly at major hotel entrances.

### Key Bus Routes

- **6001** — Myeongdong, Namdaemun, Seoul Station area
- **6002** — Gangnam, COEX area
- **6015** — Myeongdong, Dongdaemun area
- **6005** — Itaewon, Yongsan area

**How to find the bus stops:** After exiting arrivals, follow signs to the airport bus platforms outside the terminal. Ticket counters and machines are located near the bus stops. You can also pay with a T-money card when boarding.

**The catch:** Buses are subject to Seoul traffic. During rush hour (7–10 AM, 5–8 PM), a 60-minute ride can stretch to 90+ minutes. The AREX train is not affected by traffic.

## Private Transfer — Best for Groups and Late Arrivals

Private airport transfers are pre-booked car services that pick you up at the arrivals hall and drive you directly to your hotel. They cost $50–90+ depending on vehicle type and destination.

**When it makes sense:**
- You are traveling as a group of 3–4 people (splitting the cost makes it comparable to bus fare per person)
- You arrive very late at night (after midnight, when AREX and bus services are limited)
- You have a lot of luggage and want a door-to-door ride without transfers
- You are traveling with small children or elderly family members

**How to book:** You can book through Klook, Viator, or your hotel concierge. Most services let you book in advance and include a driver holding a sign with your name at the arrivals exit. Make sure to specify which terminal you are arriving at.

## Which Option Is Right for You?

| If you are... | Take... | Why |
|---|---|---|
| A solo traveler or couple on a budget | **AREX All-Stop** | Cheapest, fastest, most reliable |
| Staying in Hongdae | **AREX All-Stop** | Direct stop at Hongik Univ. Station — no transfer |
| Staying in Myeongdong | **AREX All-Stop to Seoul Stn** | Transfer to Line 4, one stop to Myeongdong |
| Carrying heavy luggage, hotel far from subway | **Airport Bus** | Door-to-door, no stairs or transfers |
| Arriving after midnight | **Private Transfer** | AREX stops running late evening |
| A family or group of 3–4 | **Private Transfer** | Split cost makes it affordable per person |

For most first-time visitors, the **AREX All-Stop is the right answer**. It is cheap, frequent, and connects directly to Seoul's subway. You do not need to pre-book anything — just show up, tap your T-money card, and go.

## Before You Leave the Airport

A few things to handle before you head into Seoul:

- **Activate your eSIM.** Turn off airplane mode and your Korea eSIM should connect immediately. You will need data for maps and messaging. If you do not have an eSIM yet, read our [Korea eSIM vs Pocket WiFi](/basics/esim-vs-pocket-wifi/) guide.
- **Get a T-money card.** You can buy one at convenience stores (CU, GS25) in the arrivals hall. Load it with 20,000–30,000 KRW for a few days of subway and bus rides.
- **Know your hotel's nearest subway station.** If you are staying in Myeongdong, Hongdae, or any other major area, check which station you need before boarding the AREX. Our [Where to Stay in Seoul](/seoul/where-to-stay/) guide lists the nearest station for each neighborhood.
