## How to Choose Your Area

Seoul is huge, but first-time visitors only need to consider a few neighborhoods. The city's subway system connects everything, so your choice of area is mostly about what you want to walk to after dinner — not about access.

Here are the four areas worth considering, compared on the things that actually matter.

## Myeongdong — Best for First-Time Visitors Who Want Everything Walkable

Myeongdong is the most popular area for tourists in Seoul, and for good reason. It sits right in the center of the city, surrounded by major subway lines, and you can walk to Namsan Tower, Namdaemun Market, and Cheonggyecheon Stream without taking a train.

The area is packed with restaurants, street food stalls, and shops. If this is your first trip and you want the least friction, Myeongdong is the safest pick.

**Best for:** First-time visitors, couples, shoppers, anyone who wants a central base.

**Not ideal for:** Travelers who dislike crowds or want a quiet neighborhood feel. Myeongdong is always busy, especially on weekends and evenings.

**Budget:** Budget hotels start around $80/night. Mid-range options run $120–180. A few luxury properties are in the $200+ range.

**Getting there from ICN Airport:** Take the AREX to Seoul Station (43 min), then transfer to Line 4 — one stop to Myeongdong Station. Total time: about 70 minutes.

## Hongdae — Best for Nightlife, Cafes, and Budget Stays

Hongdae (short for the area around Hongik University) is Seoul's creative district. It has more bars, live music venues, and independent cafes per block than anywhere else in the city. It is also one of the most affordable areas for tourists.

If you are in your 20s or 30s, traveling solo, or just want a neighborhood that stays alive past midnight, Hongdae is the right choice.

**Best for:** Solo travelers, younger travelers, nightlife seekers, budget-conscious visitors.

**Not ideal for:** Families with young children, or anyone who wants a quiet hotel after 10 PM. The streets around the main Hongdae intersection get loud on weekends.

**Budget:** Budget hostels and guesthouses from $40/night. Mid-range hotels run $80–150. Fewer luxury options than Myeongdong.

**Getting there from ICN Airport:** AREX direct train to Hongik University Station (51 min, no transfer needed). This is actually the most convenient airport connection of any Seoul neighborhood.

## Insadong / Jongno — Best for Culture and Palace Access

If you want to be walking distance from Gyeongbokgung Palace, Bukchon Hanok Village, and Changdeokgung Secret Garden, stay in the Insadong or Jongno area. This part of Seoul feels more traditional — tea houses, art galleries, and hanok-style guesthouses replace the neon shopping streets.

**Best for:** Culture-focused travelers, history lovers, couples who want a quieter atmosphere, anyone visiting palaces.

**Not ideal for:** Nightlife seekers — this area quiets down early. Shopping options are limited compared to Myeongdong or Hongdae.

**Budget:** Hanok guesthouses start around $60/night but can be basic. Standard hotels run $80–150. The area has fewer large hotel chains than Myeongdong.

**Getting there from ICN Airport:** AREX to Seoul Station, transfer to Line 3, exit at Anguk Station. About 80 minutes total.

## Gangnam — Best for Business and Upscale Dining

Gangnam sits south of the Han River, away from most of Seoul's tourist sights. It is a business and entertainment district — modern, upscale, and spread out. Unless you have a specific reason to stay in Gangnam (a conference, a meeting, or a strong preference for high-end dining), there are better choices for sightseeing trips.

**Best for:** Business travelers, luxury seekers, K-pop fans visiting HYBE or SM Entertainment areas, repeat visitors who have already done the sightseeing.

**Not ideal for:** First-time visitors focused on palaces, markets, and traditional culture — you will spend a lot of time on the subway crossing the river.

**Budget:** Mid-range starts around $100/night. Luxury hotels run $200–400. Budget options exist but are farther from the main Gangnam strip.

## Our Recommendation

For most first-time visitors, the decision comes down to **Myeongdong or Hongdae**.

- Pick **Myeongdong** if you want to be in the center of everything, close to major sights, and you do not care about nightlife.
- Pick **Hongdae** if you want a livelier neighborhood, lower prices, and the most convenient airport connection.
- Pick **Insadong** if palaces and traditional culture are your top priority.
- Skip **Gangnam** unless you have a specific reason to be south of the river.

No matter which area you choose, Seoul's subway will get you anywhere in the city in 30–40 minutes. You cannot make a bad choice — only a more or less convenient one.

## Before You Book

A few things to sort out before you lock in your hotel:

- **Get an eSIM before you land.** You will need data from the moment you arrive at ICN. See our [Korea eSIM vs Pocket WiFi](/basics/esim-vs-pocket-wifi/) guide.
- **Book your airport transfer.** The AREX train is cheap and fast, but it helps to know your route before you land.
- **Check your dates.** Cherry blossom season (late March–mid April) and fall foliage (October–November) are peak seasons. Hotel prices jump 30–50% during these periods.
