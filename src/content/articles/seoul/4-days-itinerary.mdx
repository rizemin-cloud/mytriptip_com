## Why This 4-Day Plan Works

A bad first Seoul itinerary usually has one problem: too much zig-zagging. People try to do palaces, Hongdae, Gangnam, and a day trip all in the wrong order, then lose hours to transit and tired decision-making.

This version is built around neighborhoods that naturally fit together. It is not the most aggressive list of attractions. It is the plan most people will actually enjoy following.

## Day 1: Arrive, Settle In, and Keep It Easy

If you are landing the same day, keep Day 1 light. The goal is not to "start sightseeing hard." The goal is to arrive, get your bearings, and avoid turning your first evening into a logistics puzzle.

The simplest Day 1 base for most visitors is [Myeongdong](/seoul/where-to-stay/). From there:

- Check into your hotel
- Get food nearby
- Walk through Myeongdong streets
- Head up to Namsan Seoul Tower if energy allows
- Sleep early enough that Day 2 feels good

If you stay in Hongdae instead, the same logic applies: use the first day to settle in rather than trying to cross the city repeatedly.

## Day 2: Palaces, Bukchon, and Insadong

This is your classic Seoul culture day. Grouping these sights together makes the city feel much more manageable.

Suggested flow:

1. Gyeongbokgung Palace in the morning
2. Bukchon Hanok Village before it gets too crowded
3. Insadong for lunch and slower walking
4. Optional Changdeokgung or Ikseondong in the afternoon

Why this works: all of these stops sit in the same broad zone. You are not spending the day underground on subway lines. You are actually seeing the city on foot.

## Day 3: DMZ or a Major Day Trip

For many first-time visitors, Day 3 is the best day for a structured excursion because you are settled in but not yet exhausted.

If the DMZ is on your list, put it here. Our view is simple: if you only do one major bookable tour from Seoul, the [DMZ is usually the most distinctive one](/seoul/best-dmz-tours/).

If you are not interested in the DMZ, use this day for a major city district you skipped or a different day trip. Just do not overload both Day 2 and Day 3 with early starts unless you know your travel style can handle it.

## Day 4: Pick Your Ending Based on Your Style

This is where many itineraries become too generic. A good last day depends on who you are.

### If you want cafes, younger nightlife, and shopping

Go to **Hongdae**. Walk, cafe-hop, browse local shops, and let the day stay flexible.

### If you want upscale dining or modern city energy

Go to **Gangnam**. It is not the best base for a first Seoul trip, but it can still be a worthwhile final-day district.

### If you want a slower finish

Return to a favorite area and enjoy it properly. Repeating one neighborhood you liked is often better than forcing in a new one just to tick a box.

## What Not to Do in a 4-Day Seoul Trip

- Do not change hotels mid-trip unless absolutely necessary
- Do not try to combine Seoul, Busan, and Jeju in four days
- Do not put airport arrival, palace day, and nightlife marathon all on the same day
- Do not stay far from a subway station to save a small amount on the hotel

That last one matters more than most travelers expect.

## Where to Stay for This Itinerary

If you are following this plan, the simplest bases are:

- **Myeongdong** for the easiest first trip logistics
- **Hongdae** if nightlife and direct airport access matter more
- **Insadong/Jongno** if palace access is your top priority
